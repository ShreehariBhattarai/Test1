About

An auditor's instinct, applied to Microsoft Fabric

I'm Shreehari Bhattarai, a Senior Business Intelligence Consultant based in Kathmandu, working with Dogma Group and independent clients across banking, insurance, healthcare, automotive, and trading.

My work sits at the intersection of data engineering and decision-making: I build the pipelines, models, and dashboards that let an operations lead or a CFO trust a number on a screen. That means going beyond dashboard aesthetics into the plumbing underneath — dimensional models that hold up under scale, DAX that returns the right answer under every filter combination, and refresh schedules that don't wake anyone up at 2 a.m.

I started in financial audit, which turned out to be an unusually good foundation for BI work: auditors are trained to distrust a number until they can trace where it came from, and I still approach every semantic model the same way. From there I moved into ERP consulting on Microsoft Dynamics, where I learned how transactional systems actually generate the data that later shows up in a report. That combination — knowing how the data is born, not just how to visualize it — is what I bring to every Fabric and Power BI engagement now.

Today I hold all three of Microsoft's current Fabric and Power BI certifications — DP-700 (Fabric Data Engineer Associate), DP-600 (Fabric Analytics Engineer Associate), and PL-300 (Power BI Data Analyst Associate) — and I write about what I learn implementing this stack for real clients, not just what the documentation says.
