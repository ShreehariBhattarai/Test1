Direct Lake fallback: why your "instant" report just got slow

People ask me which Power BI or Fabric certification to start with. Here's the honest answer, based on actually taking all three.

Start with PL-300

PL-300 (Power BI Data Analyst) is the right entry point if your daily work is reports and dashboards. It covers data preparation, modeling, DAX, and visualization — the core analyst skillset — without assuming you've touched Fabric's engineering side. If you already build Power BI reports for a living, this exam will feel like a structured review of habits you already have, with a few gaps filled in around row-level security and advanced DAX.

Then DP-600, not DP-700

DP-600 (Fabric Analytics Engineer) is the natural next step because it extends the analyst skillset into Fabric: lakehouses, warehouses, semantic models at scale, and Direct Lake. A lot of PL-300 knowledge transfers directly. DP-700 (Fabric Data Engineer), by contrast, leans into pipelines, notebooks, Spark, and orchestration — a genuinely different skill area, closer to data engineering than analytics.

Where each one gets hard

PL-300's hard part is usually DAX edge cases and security configuration. DP-600 gets hard around Direct Lake guardrails and capacity-aware design decisions — the kind of thing you only really learn by hitting the limits in a real workspace. DP-700 gets hard if you haven't written much Spark or PySpark before; the exam assumes comfort with notebooks that PL-300 and DP-600 don't.

The honest order

PL-300 first if you're report-focused. DP-600 second to round out the analytics-engineer skillset. DP-700 last, and only once notebooks and pipelines stop feeling unfamiliar — there's little point memorizing Spark syntax you won't otherwise use.
